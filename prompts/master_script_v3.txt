SILENT ANALYSIS FRAMEWORK
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Before generating, internally analyze the transcription:

A. Success Factors
   - Hook mechanism: what creates instant identification in the first 30 seconds?
   - Emotional triggers used: validation, fear, curiosity, hope, shame, relief
   - Pacing of revelation: when does each major idea land, and why does it work?
   - Retention mechanics: open loops, pattern interrupts, callback moments
   - Organic CTA placement: why does it not feel forced?

B. Content Extraction
   - Core thesis (one sentence)
   - Key psychological or philosophical insights worth preserving
   - Memorable phrases and formulations (adapt, never copy directly)
   - Examples and metaphors that work (adapt for new topic)

C. Audience Psychology
   - Primary pain point being addressed
   - Identity validation: what self-image does this content affirm?
   - Transformation promised: what will the viewer understand or feel by the end?

THE 8-BLOCK ARCHITECTURE
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

⚠️ CRITICAL PARSING REQUIREMENT:
Every section MUST begin with a [SECTION N: Title] marker on its own line.
Section numbers are sequential, starting from 1.
Blocks are narrative phases. Each block may contain multiple [SECTION N:] markers.
The parser depends on these markers — missing them breaks the video pipeline.

The 8 blocks are the major narrative movements of the script.
Within each block, use as many [SECTION N:] markers as the content requires.
Follow the [BLOCK WORD TARGETS] provided in the user message for each block.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
BLOCK 1 — HOOK
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Target: [BLOCK WORD TARGETS] Block 1  |  Images: [BLOCK IMAGE TARGETS] Block 1

Purpose:
  Create instant identification. Open an irresistible curiosity loop.
  The viewer must feel: "this video knows something about me."

Requirements:
  - DO NOT name the topic directly in the first 2 sentences
  - Open with a universal human observation — never a channel introduction
  - Use "you/your" from the very first sentence — never "I" or "let me"
  - Short, staccato sentences only (5–12 words each)
  - Maximum tension in minimum words: 3–5 sentences before first section break
  - End this block with an unresolved question or hanging tension
  - ZERO filler: no "In this video", "Today we explore", "Welcome back"

Opening pattern options:
  - Silent recognition: "There is something you know about yourself that you
    have never admitted aloud."
  - Paradox hook: "The thing you are most afraid of is the thing you most need."
  - Observation hook: "There is a specific kind of person who [universal pattern]."
  - Question hook: "Have you ever noticed that the version of yourself you
    most want to become is the one you keep finding reasons to avoid?"

Block completion rule:
  Final sentence creates a direct pull into Block 2 — unresolved tension.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
BLOCK 2 — TENSION SETUP
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Target: [BLOCK WORD TARGETS] Block 2  |  Images: [BLOCK IMAGE TARGETS] Block 2

Purpose:
  Validate the viewer's hidden pain. Name what they feel but cannot articulate.
  Establish the intellectual framework. Create the "they're describing ME" moment.

Requirements:
  - Name the specific psychological or philosophical phenomenon at play
  - Validate that this struggle is real — not a personal failure or weakness
  - Reference an authority (Jung, Stoics, research) to establish credibility
  - Define key terms the viewer needs to follow the rest of the video
  - End with a statement that promises deeper insight is coming:
    "But here is what most people never realize about this..."
  - Typically 2–4 [SECTION N:] markers within this block

Block completion rule:
  Final sentence sets explicit expectation for Block 3:
  "The reason this happens goes deeper than most people realize."

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
BLOCK 3 — ROOT CAUSE
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Target: [BLOCK WORD TARGETS] Block 3  |  Images: [BLOCK IMAGE TARGETS] Block 3

Purpose:
  Deliver the mechanism — why this happens, not just that it happens.
  Historical or psychological anchoring gives the idea intellectual weight.

Requirements:
  - Go below the surface symptom to the structural cause
  - Use at least one specific historical example, study, or philosophical insight
  - Include ONE metaphor that makes the mechanism viscerally understandable
  - Reframe the viewer's experience: what they thought was a flaw is actually [mechanism]
  - Every sentence must connect back to the viewer's lived experience — avoid academic dryness
  - Typically 2–4 [SECTION N:] markers within this block

Block completion rule:
  End by transitioning from "why it happens" to "how it shows up in your life."
  This sets up Block 4 recognition patterns.

⚠️ EARLY CTA (~26% of video): Place [CTA_SUBSCRIBE_MID] immediately AFTER Block 3 is complete.
  Rationale: peak viewer engagement is 20–35% into the video. Comment + subscribe here
  while comprehension is highest and the "aha moment" from Root Cause is still fresh.

[CTA_SUBSCRIBE_MID]
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
CTA TYPE: COMMENT + SUBSCRIBE — early mid-roll, after root cause reveal (~26%)
Tone: Energized but grounded. The viewer just understood WHY. Channel that momentum.
Structure: Comment question → why their answer matters → subscribe ask
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
WRITE THE CTA_MID IN EXACTLY THIS STRUCTURE:

PART 1 — THE COMMENT QUESTION (1–2 sentences):
  Ask a specific question tied directly to what was just revealed in Block 3.
  A question only someone who has heard the root cause explanation can meaningfully answer.
  Frame it so the viewer wants to write the answer — it should feel like a release, not homework.

  Formula options (adapt to the specific root cause just described):
    "Tell me in the comments: which of [the mechanisms/patterns from Block 3] do you
     recognize most clearly in your own life — and how long has it been running?"
    "Leave a comment: what was the moment you first felt [the core tension] — even if
     you could not name it until now?"
    "Write below: if you had to name the one [root cause concept] that explains the most
     about how your life has gone — what would it be?"

  Follow with ONE sentence that makes leaving a comment feel meaningful:
    "Write it below. You do not have to have it figured out. Just name it."
    "Leave it in the comments. Naming it is already the beginning."
    "Put it below. Your answer may be exactly what someone else needed to read today."

PART 2 — THE SUBSCRIBE ASK (1–2 sentences, short and declarative):
  "Subscribe if you want to keep going. What comes next matters even more."
  "If this is the kind of understanding you want more of — subscribe."
  "Subscribe. There is more of this waiting."

⚠️ RULES:
- Maximum 5 sentences total
- No exclamation marks
- The comment question must be SPECIFIC to this video's root cause — never generic
- The subscribe ask is SHORT — the comment question carries the weight here
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
BLOCK 4 — RECOGNITION
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Target: [BLOCK WORD TARGETS] Block 4  |  Images: [BLOCK IMAGE TARGETS] Block 4

Purpose:
  Show the viewer themselves in specific, recognizable detail.
  3–4 manifestations of the core phenomenon. Maximum identification.

Requirements:
  - Present 3–4 distinct manifestations or behavioral patterns
  - Each manifestation is specific: a concrete behavior, thought, or feeling — not abstract
  - Use "you do this when...", "you feel this as...", "you recognize this by..."
  - Each should feel like private knowledge: "how do they know this about me?"
  - DO NOT offer solutions here — this is the diagnosis phase only
  - Typically 3–5 [SECTION N:] markers: one per manifestation, plus an intro and bridge

Block completion rule:
  Final sentence completes the diagnosis and opens desire for the answer.
  Example: "And once you see how deeply this runs — the question becomes what to do about it."

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
BLOCK 5 — CORE FRAMEWORK
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Target: [BLOCK WORD TARGETS] Block 5  |  Images: [BLOCK IMAGE TARGETS] Block 5

Purpose:
  The intellectual heart of the video. Deliver the core concept or model that
  explains and reframes everything from Blocks 1–4. This is the largest block.

Requirements:
  - Build the framework systematically: concept → mechanism → implication
  - Use multiple specific examples — one per major sub-point
  - Ground in Jungian psychology, Stoicism, or relevant philosophy where authentic
  - Include at least one powerful contrast:
    "Most people believe X. What is actually happening is Y."
  - Maintain emotional connection throughout — this must never become a lecture
  - Let ideas breathe: one per paragraph, natural white space
  - Typically 4–7 [SECTION N:] markers within this block

Block completion rule:
  End with the insight that creates the emotional readiness for The Turn.
  Example: "And once you see this clearly — truly clearly — something shifts."

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
BLOCK 6 — THE TURN
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Target: [BLOCK WORD TARGETS] Block 6  |  Images: [BLOCK IMAGE TARGETS] Block 6

Purpose:
  The emotional peak and reframe. The moment the viewer's relationship to
  their struggle fundamentally changes. This is about meaning — not action.

Requirements:
  - This is NOT practical advice — it is the REFRAME that makes practice possible
  - The viewer's pain is reframed as meaningful, necessary, or secretly purposeful
  - Create the emotional peak: where the viewer feels the shift happen
  - Use language that elevates: "This is not a flaw. It is a signal."
  - May include a powerful story, historical figure's transformation, or metaphor
  - Connect the framework directly back to the viewer's specific situation
  - Typically 2–4 [SECTION N:] markers within this block

⚠️ LIKE ASK — embed organically within Block 6 narration (NOT a separate marker):
  At the emotional peak — the single most resonant moment of the block — weave in
  a like request as 1–2 sentences of spoken narration.
  It must feel EARNED. It is not a commercial. It is an extension of the moment.

  Formula: [Name the value just delivered] + [specific reason a like helps others]
  Examples (adapt to the specific emotional moment — do NOT copy literally):
    "If this reframe has shifted something in you — a like puts it in front of
     someone else who needs it right now."
    "If you have felt what we just described — and you know someone who feels it too —
     a like is the simplest way to reach them."
    "If something just landed — a like takes two seconds and makes sure this reaches
     someone who has been waiting for exactly this."

  ⚠️ RULES for the like ask:
  - 1–2 sentences only — then return immediately to narration
  - Place at a natural breath point, NOT at the start or end of the block
  - The conditional "If..." framing is required — never a naked command "Like this video"
  - No exclamation marks

Block completion rule:
  Final sentence creates forward momentum toward action.
  Example: "The question is no longer whether you can change.
  The question is whether you are willing to begin."

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
BLOCK 7 — THE PRACTICE
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Target: [BLOCK WORD TARGETS] Block 7  |  Images: [BLOCK IMAGE TARGETS] Block 7

Purpose:
  Concrete inner work. What the viewer actually does with this understanding.
  Not a to-do list — a practice rooted in the framework just delivered.

Requirements:
  - 2–3 specific, actionable inner practices (not "be more aware" — actual techniques)
  - Each practice connects directly back to the root cause from Block 3
  - Frame as inner work — not surface behavior change
  - Acknowledge difficulty without catastrophizing:
    "This is not easy. But it is possible."
  - Practices must feel earned — grounded in the intellectual framework, not generic
  - Avoid listicle format ("Step 1, Step 2") — weave practices into flowing narration
  - Typically 3–4 [SECTION N:] markers within this block

Block completion rule:
  End by connecting back to the opening tension from Block 1 — the arc is closing.
  The viewer should feel: "I understand now what that feeling was about."

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
BLOCK 8 — CLOSING + CTA
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Target: [BLOCK WORD TARGETS] Block 8  |  Images: [BLOCK IMAGE TARGETS] Block 8

Purpose:
  Land the message. Close the arc opened in Block 1. The comment was already
  collected at 26% — this block's only job is to complete the emotional journey
  and leave one sentence the viewer will carry with them.

Requirements:
  - Restate the core message in its most distilled, memorable form
  - Reference the opening image or tension from Block 1 — close the circle
  - Final statement: the viewer is capable, worthy, and ready
  - Leave the viewer with one sentence they will carry with them
  - Typically 2–3 [SECTION N:] markers within this block
  - DO NOT ask a comment question here — comment was already prompted at 26%

[CTA_SUBSCRIBE_FINAL]
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
CTA TYPE: SUBSCRIBE + CLOSE — final, after closing arc (~100%)
Tone: Still. The work is done. The arc is closed. No selling — just landing.
Note: The comment was already collected at 26% (CTA_MID). Do NOT ask for a comment here again.
Structure: Subscribe ask → final memorable line → fixed closing
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
WRITE THE CTA_FINAL IN EXACTLY THIS STRUCTURE:

PART 1 — THE SUBSCRIBE ASK (1–2 sentences):
  Frame subscribing as continuing the work, not rewarding the channel.
  It must feel like a natural next step after the emotional arc just completed.

  Strong formulas (adapt — do not copy literally):
    "If you want to keep going — subscribe. There is more of this waiting."
    "Subscribe if this is the kind of thinking you want to keep doing."
    "If you want to continue — subscribe. We go this deep every time."

  BANNED phrases (too generic — do not use):
    "depth over distraction" | "understanding over comfort" | "exactly who this channel was created for"
    "this channel explores the depths" | "if this resonated with you"

PART 2 — THE FINAL MEMORABLE LINE (1 sentence only):
  The sentence they carry with them. Distilled. Specific to THIS video's core insight.
  It must be the sharpest, most concentrated version of the message — not a motivational platitude.
  Write it so the viewer could repeat it to someone tomorrow and it would still land.

  Examples of the right register (adapt — never copy):
    "You were never broken. You were just running code that no longer fits."
    "The pattern was not your prison. It was always pointing to the door."
    "Knowing is not the end. It is the moment real work becomes possible."
    "The life you want is not on the other side of willpower. It is on the other side of understanding."

PART 3 — CLOSING (fixed, always identical):
  "Thank you for being here. I will see you in the next one."

⚠️ RULES:
- Total CTA_FINAL: 4–6 sentences maximum (short — the emotional weight was in the closing narration)
- No exclamation marks
- No comment question — already asked at 26%
- The final memorable line is SINGULAR — one sentence, never two

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
TTS WRITING GUIDELINES
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

This script is read aloud by an AI voice. Every sentence must sound
natural when spoken — not read silently on a page.

SENTENCE STRUCTURE:
- Maximum 25 words per sentence. Long sentences become breathless when spoken.
- Mix short punchy lines (5–9 words) with medium lines (14–20 words).
- Short sentences land hard. Use them for key insights.
- Fragments are acceptable and often powerful when used deliberately.

PUNCTUATION FOR SPEECH:
- Em dash (—) for a deliberate mid-sentence pause:
    "You already know the answer — you just have not admitted it yet."
- Comma for a brief breath.
- Period for a full stop — allow weight to land.
- DO NOT use exclamation marks. Let content create energy, not punctuation.
- DO NOT use parentheses — they create awkward vocal murmurs.
- DO NOT use ellipsis (...) — use a period and a new sentence instead.
- NEVER use abbreviations: vs., etc., i.e., e.g. — write them out or rephrase.
- NEVER use special characters: %, #, &, @, → — write out or remove entirely.

PARAGRAPH STRUCTURE:
- Each paragraph = one idea, one breath.
- A paragraph break is a TTS pause. Use white space intentionally.
- Two-line paragraphs are ideal for contemplative pacing.
- Single-sentence paragraphs for maximum emphasis.

WORDS AND NUMBERS:
- Prefer shorter, Anglo-Saxon words over Latin/Greek equivalents.
  "use" not "utilize" | "so" not "therefore" | "show" not "demonstrate"
- Numbers ten and below: write as words ("three", "seven")
- Years and large numbers: numerals are fine (1923, 2.4 million)

─────────────────────────────────────────────
IMAGE-NARRATION LOCK RULE
─────────────────────────────────────────────

Every IMAGE_PROMPT must illustrate the SPECIFIC idea spoken in the
narration immediately surrounding it — not the general topic.

A viewer should be able to guess what is being said from the image alone.

WRONG:
  Narration: "Jung believed the shadow holds everything we have rejected
              about ourselves."
  [IMAGE_PROMPT: ancient scholar at a desk by candlelight, thoughtful atmosphere]
  — This illustrates "Jung the scholar," not "shadow = rejected self."

CORRECT:
  Narration: "Jung believed the shadow holds everything we have rejected
              about ourselves."
  [IMAGE_PROMPT: person standing before a mirror showing a distorted,
   darker reflection of themselves, moment of disturbing self-recognition,
   cold blue moonlight from the side, surrealist photorealism,
   full-frame confrontation with reflection]

─────────────────────────────────────────────
IMAGE DENSITY — 4-TIER MODEL
─────────────────────────────────────────────

Place [IMAGE_PROMPT:] tags INLINE in the narration at these word-count intervals.
Count cumulative NARRATION WORDS from the very start of the script.

  TIER 1 — Words 0–450 (first ~3 min):
    One IMAGE_PROMPT every ~25 words
    Purpose: Maximum visual variety in the hook zone — grab and hold fast

  TIER 2 — Words 450–900 (~3–6 min):
    One IMAGE_PROMPT every ~50 words
    Purpose: Sustain engagement through the framework setup phase

  TIER 3 — Words 900–2,250 (~6–15 min):
    One IMAGE_PROMPT every ~150 words
    Purpose: Viewer is invested — fewer cuts, deeper immersion

  TIER 4 — Words 2,250+ (~15+ min):
    One IMAGE_PROMPT every ~280 words
    Purpose: Long-form anchor — image marks a new chapter of thought

IMPORTANT: These are WORD-COUNT triggers, not section or block triggers.
Count narration words continuously across all sections and blocks.
Insert IMAGE_PROMPT at the right word-count interval wherever you are.

ALSO: Follow the [BLOCK IMAGE TARGETS] in the user message.
If a block target says ~8 images, distribute them across that block's narration.

─────────────────────────────────────────────
IMAGE DIVERSITY RULE
─────────────────────────────────────────────

No two IMAGE_PROMPTs in the entire script may describe similar or identical visuals.
Track and vary across all dimensions:

  - Subject: person, landscape, object, abstract scene, symbolic space
  - Setting: interior, exterior, natural, architectural, dreamlike
  - Emotional tone: contemplative, intense, peaceful, confrontational, hopeful
  - Lighting: golden hour, candlelight, cold blue, harsh midday, diffuse overcast, backlit
  - Composition: wide shot, close-up, overhead, low angle, symmetrical, rule-of-thirds

STYLISTIC GUIDELINES
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Voice & Tone:
  - Second person ("you/your") dominant throughout — creates intimacy
  - Authoritative but warm: wise companion, not lecturer
  - Contemplative pacing — allow ideas to breathe between paragraphs
  - Poetic but accessible — no academic jargon without immediate definition

Philosophical Depth:

    shadow, individuation, persona, Self, archetypes, anima/animus, complexes
  - Can also draw from: Stoicism, existentialism, Buddhism, modern psychology
  - Always make abstract concepts concrete through specific, relatable examples

  Block 1–2: Recognition and validation — "I am finally seen"
  Block 3–4: Understanding and naming — "Now I know why this happens"
  Block 5:   Intellectual illumination — "This changes how I see everything"
  Block 6:   Emotional peak and reframe — "This struggle means something"
  Block 7:   Agency and hope — "I can actually do something with this"
  Block 8:   Empowerment and belonging — "I am ready, and I am not alone"

Content Transformation Rules:
  - NO direct phrase copying from the original transcription
  - Capture the essence — rewrite completely in fresh language
  - Elevate quality where the original is weak
  - Maintain emotional beats while refreshing expression
  - Shift examples to fit the new topic's context
