# HOOKS REFERENCE GUIDE
## For VideoForge Script Generator — Condensed Actionable Version

---

## CORE 3-STEP FORMULA (use for every hook)

**Step 1 — Context Lean-In** (1-2 sentences)
Give crystal-clear topic clarity + lean the viewer in. Methods:
- State a surprising fact or counterintuitive claim
- Point to a pain point the viewer has
- Establish common ground ("If you've ever felt...")

**Step 2 — Scroll-Stop Interjection** (1 sentence)
A single contrasting phrase that acts as a wall the viewer runs into:
- "But..." / "However..." / "Yet..." / "Except..."

**Step 3 — Contrarian Snapback** (1-2 sentences)
Reverse the direction completely. The bigger the reversal, the stronger the hook.

**Example:**
> "The people who command the most respect never try to earn it. They never explain themselves, justify their choices, or rush to soothe discomfort. *But here's what psychology discovered about why this works* — and it changes how you'll see every relationship you have."

---

## 6 HOOK TYPES

**1. Curiosity Hook** — Create a gap between what they know and want to know.
> "There is a name for what you've been experiencing — Jung identified it over a century ago, and almost no one talks about it."

**2. Storytelling Hook** — Authentic personal story creates emotional connection.
> "Three years ago I walked away from the only person who had ever truly seen me. Here's what I learned from losing everything."

**3. Challenge Hook** — Specific goal + constraint creates tension.
> "I decided to spend 30 days doing nothing I didn't genuinely want to do. No explaining. No justifying. The results were uncomfortable."

**4. Negative Hook** — Pain points through negative lens (highest viral potential).
> "You're destroying your relationships without realizing it — and the method is called being too available."

**5. Comparison Hook** — Two things + clear verdict = engaged viewer.
> "Desperate people chase approval. Psychologically integrated people attract it without trying. Here's what separates them."

**6. Observation Hook** — Describe a type of person the viewer recognizes as themselves.
> "There is a certain kind of person who gives endlessly — and is taken for granted endlessly. This video is for them."

---

## SSSQ RULE
**Single Subject, Single Question** — every hook must:
- Focus on ONE clear topic (zero ambiguity)
- Plant ONE specific question in the viewer's mind

Diagnostic: *If 50,000 people watched, would they all wonder the same thing?*

---

## 4 DEADLY MISTAKES

1. **Starting with "In this video..."** — instant skip
2. **No clear subject** — viewer doesn't know what they're watching
3. **Burying the hook** — putting the interesting part at 0:45 instead of 0:05
4. **Over-promising** — claiming transformation without any specificity

---

## HOOK CHECKLIST

Before finalizing the hook block, verify:
- [ ] Viewer knows exactly what this video is about within 5 seconds
- [ ] There is a clear curiosity gap ("I need to keep watching to find out...")
- [ ] Uses "you" language — speaks directly to the viewer
- [ ] Has a pattern interrupt (contrast, "but", reversal)
- [ ] No generic opener ("Hey guys", "Welcome back", "In today's video")
- [ ] Under 75 words for the first spoken sentence group

---

## NICHE-SPECIFIC ANGLE (Psychology / Philosophy / Self-Development)

Lead with the psychological/philosophical tension:
- Name a concept most people haven't heard of: "Jung called this the *persona trap*"
- Validate the viewer's unspoken experience: "You have felt this — you just never had words for it"
- Promise a reframe: "By the end of this, you'll see [X] completely differently"

The emotional arc: **Recognition → Validation → Insight → Reframe → Empowerment**
