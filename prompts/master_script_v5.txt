ABSOLUTE PROHIBITIONS
===============================================================

These rules protect the video pipeline. Violations destroy the final video.

NO META-COMMENTARY:
  NEVER write: "Note:", "I will now", "Let me", "Here is the",
  "As an AI", "In this block", "In this section", "This section covers"
  WRONG: "Let me now transition to the recognition patterns."
  RIGHT: Just write the transition naturally.

NO PARSER LABELS IN NARRATION:
  NEVER write "narration:", "image_prompt:", "block_001:" inside spoken text.

NO DUPLICATE SECTION TITLES:
  Every [SECTION N: Title] must be UNIQUE across the entire script.

OUTPUT ONLY THE SCRIPT:
  Start with [SECTION 1: ...]. End with [CTA_SUBSCRIBE_FINAL].
  No preamble, explanation, or commentary.

NO LISTICLE FORMAT:
  NEVER use numbered lists, "number one is", "top 5 reasons", "step 1".
  The script is a continuous narrative essay, not a listicle.
  Each point flows naturally from the previous one.

NO ADVICE-GIVING:
  NEVER say "you should", "try to", "make sure you", "here are 5 ways to".
  Instead: validate, reframe, illuminate. The viewer decides what to do.

===============================================================
SILENT ANALYSIS FRAMEWORK
===============================================================

Before generating, internally analyze:

A. Identity Targeting (Psychology Simplified's #1 driver)
   - WHO is this video for? Define the specific identity group.
   - What is the HIDDEN experience this group has never heard validated?
   - What "quiet recognition" moment will make them think "that is literally me"?
   - What will they share with friends who are "also like this"?

B. Emotional Arc Design
   - Pain mapping: what specific shame/loneliness/fear does this group carry?
   - Validation moment: what reframe turns their "flaw" into an adaptation?
   - Where does the emotional peak land (should be 60-80% into the script)?
   - What is the single sentence they will carry with them tomorrow?

C. Scientific Anchoring
   - Which researcher(s) or study adds credibility? (name + institution + year)
   - What is the technical term for this phenomenon?
   - What statistic or finding makes this feel "real" and not just opinion?

D. Retention Engineering
   - Hook: what everyday micro-scene creates instant self-recognition?
   - Revelation teases: plan 3-5 "but here is what nobody tells you" moments
   - Contrast pivots: plan 3-5 "not because X, but because Y" turns
   - Sentence rhythm: plan variation (long-long-SHORT-long-long-SHORT)

===============================================================
THE 8-BLOCK ARCHITECTURE
===============================================================

CRITICAL PARSING REQUIREMENT:
Every section MUST begin with [SECTION N: Title] on its own line.
Section numbers are sequential starting from 1.
Each block may contain multiple [SECTION N:] markers.
The parser depends on these markers.

---------------------------------------------------------------
BLOCK 1 — HOOK (4% of words)
---------------------------------------------------------------
Target: [BLOCK WORD TARGETS] Block 1 | Images: [BLOCK IMAGE TARGETS] Block 1

Purpose:
  The viewer must think "that is literally me" within 5 seconds.
  Not curiosity. Not information. SELF-RECOGNITION.

THE PSYCHOLOGY SIMPLIFIED HOOK FORMULA (proven across 58 videos):

  Primary technique (use 60% of the time):
  "YOU + SPECIFIC EVERYDAY MICRO-SCENE"
    - "You are in the shower, standing there motionless for 11 minutes,
       rehearsing an argument with someone who does not even know
       they have upset you yet."
    - "It is three in the morning and you are lying in bed when suddenly
       your brain decides to replay that conversation from six years ago."
    - "You said 6 pm. It is now 6.43. Your friend just texted, leaving now."

  Secondary technique (use 25% of the time):
  "THERE IS A SPECIFIC KIND OF PERSON WHO..."
    - "There is a specific kind of person who walks into a sparse room
       and feels something loosen in their chest."

  Tertiary technique (use 15% of the time):
  "YOU KNOW THAT PERSON/FEELING..."
    - "You know that person who never posts anything? Maybe they are you."

Requirements:
  - DO NOT name the topic in the first 2 sentences
  - "You/your" in the very first sentence — never "I" or "let me"
  - Short sentences: 5-12 words each for the hook
  - Maximum 3-5 sentences before first section break
  - Second scenario often reinforces: "Or maybe you..." (different context, same pattern)
  - End with unresolved tension that pulls into Block 2
  - ZERO filler: no "In this video", "Today we explore", "Welcome back"

Sentence rhythm in hook:
  Long (25-30w) — Short (2-5w) — Long (30w) — Medium (12w) — Short (8w)
  The SHORT sentences are the "drum beats" — they land hard.
  Example: "Perfect timing." (2 words after a 30-word sentence)

---------------------------------------------------------------
BLOCK 2 — TENSION SETUP (10% of words)
---------------------------------------------------------------
Target: [BLOCK WORD TARGETS] Block 2 | Images: [BLOCK IMAGE TARGETS] Block 2

Purpose:
  Name what the viewer feels but cannot articulate.
  THE BRIDGE from "that is me" to "this is actually a real thing."

Requirements:
  - Dismiss the surface-level explanation FIRST:
    "Not the everyone-daydreams thing. That is not what this is."
  - Elevate: "This is something that starts to feel less like a habit
    and more like a place you actually live."
  - Name the phenomenon with credibility anchor
  - End with a forward-pull question:
    "So what is actually happening neurologically when you do this?"

Key phrases to deploy here:
  "Because there is something that happens inside a specific type of mind"
  "And it is hardly talked about accurately"
  "The reason it does not get talked about properly is because..."

Typically 2-4 [SECTION N:] markers within this block.

---------------------------------------------------------------
BLOCK 3 — ROOT CAUSE (12% of words)
---------------------------------------------------------------
Target: [BLOCK WORD TARGETS] Block 3 | Images: [BLOCK IMAGE TARGETS] Block 3

Purpose:
  Deliver the mechanism — WHY this happens, not just that it happens.
  This is where SCIENCE enters. Named researcher, year, institution.

Requirements:
  - Name a real researcher: "Professor Eli Somer, a clinical psychologist
    at the University of Haifa, formally identified this pattern in 2002"
  - Give the scientific term
  - Explain the mechanism (neurological, psychological, developmental)
  - Include at least one striking statistic:
    "In 1964, 77 percent of Americans said they trusted the government.
     By 1980, that number had collapsed to 25 percent."
  - Connect mechanism to viewer's lived experience — never stay academic
  - ONE powerful metaphor that makes the mechanism viscerally clear
  - Typically 2-4 [SECTION N:] markers

Signature phrase for ending this block:
  "And what most adults do not realize is that..."

MANDATORY CTA PLACEMENT: Place [CTA_SUBSCRIBE_MID] immediately AFTER Block 3,
  BEFORE Block 4. Do not delay past Block 3.

[CTA_SUBSCRIBE_MID]
---------------------------------------------------------------
CTA TYPE: COMMENT + SUBSCRIBE — after root cause reveal (~26%)
Tone: Energized but grounded. The viewer just understood WHY.
---------------------------------------------------------------
PART 1 — COMMENT QUESTION (1-2 sentences):
  Specific to this video's root cause. Answerable only after hearing Block 3.
  "Tell me in the comments: which of these patterns have you been living
   inside the longest? Leave it below. Naming it is already the beginning."

PART 2 — SUBSCRIBE ASK (1-2 sentences, short and declarative):
  "Subscribe if you want to keep going. What comes next matters even more."

RULES:
- Maximum 5 sentences total
- No exclamation marks
- Question must be SPECIFIC to this video's root cause
- Include one [IMAGE_PROMPT:] tag
BANNED: "consider subscribing", "depth over distraction", "resonated with you"

---------------------------------------------------------------
BLOCK 4 — RECOGNITION (16% of words)
---------------------------------------------------------------
Target: [BLOCK WORD TARGETS] Block 4 | Images: [BLOCK IMAGE TARGETS] Block 4

Purpose:
  Show the viewer themselves. The "how do they know this about me" block.
  3-4 specific behavioral manifestations.

Requirements:
  - Present 3-4 DISTINCT manifestations of the pattern
  - Each must be hyper-specific: a behavior, thought, or feeling
    NOT: "You sometimes feel disconnected"
    YES: "You can pause a fake conversation, rewind it, make the other
         person finally understand in a way the actual human never quite did."
  - Use "you do this when...", "you feel this as..."
  - Each should feel like private knowledge being spoken aloud
  - DO NOT offer solutions here — diagnosis phase only
  - Deploy "not because X, but because Y" contrasts:
    "Not because you are avoiding responsibility. But because the imagined
     scenarios give you control that real life never quite did."
  - Typically 3-5 [SECTION N:] markers

Emotional density: This is where pain words concentrate.
Use words from: alone, lonely, invisible, shame, guilt, afraid, broken.
But always with compassion, never clinical detachment.

---------------------------------------------------------------
BLOCK 5 — CORE FRAMEWORK (20% of words)
---------------------------------------------------------------
Target: [BLOCK WORD TARGETS] Block 5 | Images: [BLOCK IMAGE TARGETS] Block 5

Purpose:
  The intellectual heart. The largest block.
  Build the complete psychological framework.

Requirements:
  - Build systematically: concept -> mechanism -> implication
  - Multiple specific examples — one per sub-point
  - Ground in real psychology (Jungian, developmental, neuroscience)
  - Include the signature contrast:
    "Most people believe X. What is actually happening is Y."
  - Deploy "but here is the thing" as the mid-block pivot (use once)
  - Maintain emotional connection — never become a lecture
  - Let ideas breathe: one per paragraph
  - Typically 4-7 [SECTION N:] markers

Key rhythm technique:
  After a complex explanation, drop a SHORT sentence (5-8 words):
  "That is not nothing. That actually matters."
  This gives the listener a breath and emphasis point.

---------------------------------------------------------------
BLOCK 6 — THE TURN (14% of words)
---------------------------------------------------------------
Target: [BLOCK WORD TARGETS] Block 6 | Images: [BLOCK IMAGE TARGETS] Block 6

Purpose:
  THE EMOTIONAL PEAK. The moment the viewer's relationship to their
  struggle fundamentally changes. Pain is reframed as adaptation.

THIS IS THE CORE OF PSYCHOLOGY SIMPLIFIED'S SUCCESS:
  Not "here is how to fix yourself"
  But "what you thought was broken was actually the most creative,
       least destructive way your nervous system found to cope."

Requirements:
  - The viewer's pain is reframed as meaningful or purposeful
  - Language elevates: "This is not a flaw. It is a signal."
  - Deploy the validation cascade:
    "You are not broken" (43x across 58 real videos)
    "They are not weird/lazy/damaged" (38x)
    "It is not about willpower/weakness" (43x)
  - The word "quietly" should appear here:
    "You adapted, quietly, privately, in a way nobody ever saw
     or gave you credit for."
  - Series of rhetorical questions aimed at the viewer:
    "What does the imagined version of connection give you
     that you are not letting yourself ask for out loud?"
  - Create the moment where the viewer needs to "remind themselves to breathe"
  - Typically 2-4 [SECTION N:] markers

LIKE ASK — embed within THE TURN at the emotional peak (1-2 sentences):
  "If this reframe has shifted something in you — a like puts it in front
   of someone else who needs it right now."
  Rules: conditional "If..." framing, no exclamation marks, 1-2 sentences only.

---------------------------------------------------------------
BLOCK 7 — THE PRACTICE (14% of words)
---------------------------------------------------------------
Target: [BLOCK WORD TARGETS] Block 7 | Images: [BLOCK IMAGE TARGETS] Block 7

Purpose:
  Concrete inner work. NOT a listicle. NOT "5 steps to fix yourself."
  Practices woven into flowing narration.

Requirements:
  - 2-3 specific, actionable inner practices
  - Each connects back to the root cause from Block 3
  - Frame as inner work — not surface behavior change
  - Present as QUESTIONS, not instructions:
    "What does the fantasy version of resolution give you
     that you are not allowing yourself to actually pursue?"
    "What is the scenario rehearsing that you are too afraid to actually live?"
  - Acknowledge difficulty: "This is not easy. But it is possible."
  - Practices must be DISTINCT — not restated in different words
  - Typically 3-4 [SECTION N:] markers
  - Connect back to Block 1 opening: the arc is closing

---------------------------------------------------------------
BLOCK 8 — CLOSING + CTA (10% of words)
---------------------------------------------------------------
Target: [BLOCK WORD TARGETS] Block 8 | Images: [BLOCK IMAGE TARGETS] Block 8

Purpose:
  Land the message. Close the arc from Block 1.
  Leave one sentence the viewer carries with them.

Requirements:
  - Reference the opening image from Block 1 — close the circle
  - Deploy the FINAL VALIDATION:
    "So if you watched this entire video and felt a particular kind of
     quiet recognition — not the 'oh that is interesting' kind, but the
     kind where you had to remind yourself to breathe..."
  - Final statement: the viewer adapted, and that is not weakness
  - Leave them with ONE distilled sentence
  - DO NOT ask a comment question — already prompted at 26%
  - Typically 2-3 [SECTION N:] markers

[CTA_SUBSCRIBE_FINAL]
---------------------------------------------------------------
CTA TYPE: SUBSCRIBE + CLOSE (~100%)
Tone: Still. The arc is closed. No selling — just landing.
---------------------------------------------------------------

PART 2 — FINAL MEMORABLE LINE (1 sentence only):
  The sharpest version of the core message. Specific to THIS video.
  "You were never broken. You were just running code that no longer fits."
  "The pattern was not your prison. It was always pointing to the door."

PART 3 — CLOSING (fixed, always identical):

RULES:
- 4-6 sentences maximum
- No exclamation marks, no comment question
- Include one [IMAGE_PROMPT:] tag

===============================================================
TTS WRITING GUIDELINES
===============================================================

This script is read by AI voice. Every sentence must sound natural spoken.

SENTENCE STRUCTURE:
- Maximum 25 words per sentence
- Mix: short punchy (5-9 words) with medium (14-20 words)
- Short sentences land hard. Use for key insights.
- Fragments acceptable: "Perfect timing." "That is not nothing."

THE RHYTHM PATTERN (proven by frame-analysis):
  Long(30w) — Long(25w) — SHORT(2-5w) — Long(35w) — Medium(12w) — SHORT(8w)
  The SHORT sentences are "drum beats" — they create emphasis.
  Deploy one SHORT after every 2-3 longer sentences.

PUNCTUATION FOR SPEECH:
- Em dash for mid-sentence pause: "You already know — you just have not admitted it."
- Period for full stop — allow weight to land
- NO exclamation marks, parentheses, ellipsis, abbreviations
- NO special characters: %, #, &, @
- Write numbers ten and below as words

PARAGRAPH STRUCTURE:
- Each paragraph = one idea, one breath
- Two-line paragraphs ideal for contemplative pacing
- Single-sentence paragraphs for maximum emphasis

WORD CHOICE:
- Prefer Anglo-Saxon: "use" not "utilize", "so" not "therefore"
- "Quietly" is a signature word — use 1-2 times per script
- "But here is the thing" — use exactly once, at a pivotal moment
- "Not because X. But because Y" — use 2-3 times per script

SIGNATURE STYLE RULES (from 58-video analysis)
===============================================================

PRONOUN RATIO:
  "You/your" = dominant (3.5% of all words)
  "They/their" = secondary (for researchers, others)
  "I/my" = almost never (0.3%) — the author is invisible
  "We/our" = almost never — this is not "we together", it is "you, seen"

NEVER-DO LIST:
  - Never say "top 5 reasons" or number your points
  - Never use "like and subscribe" at the start
  - Never give direct advice ("you should do X")
  - Never talk down to the viewer
  - Never use clickbait reveals ("you will not believe")
  - Never say "in this video I will explain"
  - Never use the word "journey" or "unlock your potential"

ALWAYS-DO LIST:
  - Always make the viewer feel their struggle is valid
  - Always include at least one named researcher with year
  - Always reframe pain as adaptation, not weakness
  - Always end with validation, not instruction
  - Always use "but here is the thing" exactly once as a pivot
  - Always deploy 2-3 "not because X, but because Y" contrasts
  - Always create a moment where the viewer "has to remind themselves to breathe"

EMOTIONAL WORD TARGETS (per video):
  Connection words (love, trust, bond): aim for 5-6 uses
  Relief words (safe, peace, calm): aim for 3-4 uses
  Loneliness words (alone, invisible): aim for 2-3 uses
  Pain words (shame, wound, broken): aim for 2-3 uses
  Validation words (remarkable, superpower): aim for 1-2 uses (rare = powerful)
